A New Catheter Paradigm

CathPrint offers CathPrint™, a catheter design and manufacturing technology that will rationalize existing catheters or realize challenging product concepts into marketable products, thus enabling significant cost savings, improved performance and increased profitability. The technology to produce flexible printed circuit boards - flexible PCBs – has been around for a long time. This proven technology is the basis for CathPrint’s new innovative process for catheter manufacturing, CathPrint™. Instead of today's small-scale handmade manufacturing, all the wires, electrodes and contacts are put together in one semi-automatic operation.


CathPrint works closely with customers to design and produce catheters and endoscopes and has established small-scale production for customers at its premises. CathPrint is based in Stockholm, Sweden, and serves a variety of customers.

TECHNOLOGY

Driven by innovation, CathPrint AB brings to the medical industry a new and effective method of catheter production, substantially reducing manpower efforts and costs. Working with various companies in the catheter industry, CathPrint has introduced novel solutions to producing catheters with differing requirements.


For simple catheters: CathPrint™

Enables automated and rational production of catheters. Enables design and production of catheters that are more complex and advanced than those available today. Simplifies functionality and component addition to present catheters. Reduces overall production costs by more than 50%. Is strongly protected by patents and patent applications.

For composite catheter: CathPrint™ Integrated

In situations that require lumens, e.g. for liquid solutions such as medicine or nutrients, an extrusion with at least two lumens, one of which is occupied by a CathPrint™ catheter, is used. The CathPrint™ Integrated technology eliminates the need for numerous wires and significantly rationalises the production process. This arrangement also maintains flexibility of the device. CathPrint™ Integrated is a patent-protected technology.

For fast prototyping: CathPrint™ Prefab

CathPrint™ Prefab is manufactured with use of standard flexible PCB’s and rational electronics production processes. It is inserted into the lumen that normally is used for the wires connecting electrodes, sensors or other electronics. The product is based on CathPrint’s patented technology. In production, the Prefab is inserted into a lumen of the customer’s extrusion that is prepared with holes at the positions of electrodes and pre-mounted sensors. Electrodes are connected by conductive glue and sensors are covered by a protecting gel. The distal tip is optional and can be used to pre-mount customer sensors/electronics or be equipped with pads for wire-bonding. CathPrint™ Prefab can be delivered for a variety of catheters. The length can be up to 120 cm and the number of electrodes and sensors can be up to 20 depending on the length and diameter of the Prefab.

BUSINESS

CathPrint offers its technology to companies that market, sell and manufacture catheters or endoscopes (product owners). The technology can be used to rationalized existing products or develop new advanced products that are difficult to manufacture with current technology.

CathPrint plans for three different modes of operation:

OEM design and production to small and medium sized companies.
This is the way we are working with our current customers and prospects.
CathPrint develops and adapts the catheter production process together with the
customers and production will be done by us or with the aid of a production partner.
Packaged and sterilized catheters are delivered to the customer.

Licensing our technology to companies who manage their production themselves.
In this case the customer develops and adapts the catheter production process with
the aid of CathPrint. Production is established at the customers’ site using production
equipment supplied by CathPrint, who will also support the production establishment.
The client pays for the establishment and training costs, and also royalty per produced catheter.

In the long run we plan to develop own products by support of our world leading medical network.
This may also be as joint ventures with other companies.
